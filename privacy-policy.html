Privacy Policy

Effective Date: October 15, 2025

This Privacy Policy ("Policy") governs the manner in which LIYANA LOGISTICS USA INC. ("Company," "we," "us," or "our"), a corporation organized under the laws of the State of New York, collects, uses, maintains, discloses, and protects personal information provided by users ("you" or "your") through our website and in connection with the international and domestic logistics and freight forwarding services we provide.

This Policy is intended to comply with applicable privacy and data protection laws, including the New York Stop Hacks and Improve Electronic Data Security Act (SHIELD Act), the Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA), the CAN-SPAM Act, and other applicable federal and state privacy regulations.

By accessing our website, engaging with our services, or otherwise providing us with your personal information, you acknowledge and agree to the terms and conditions of this Privacy Policy.

1. Information We Collect

We may collect the following categories of personal information from or about you, either directly or indirectly:

1.1 Identifiers and Contact Information

  • Full name
  • Company name and business role
  • Mailing address and/or physical address
  • Email address
  • Telephone numbers (including mobile)
  • Government-issued identification or tax identification numbers (as required by law)

1.2 Commercial and Transactional Information

  • Shipment contents and cargo descriptions
  • Origin and destination addresses
  • Shipping documents, including invoices, packing lists, and bills of lading
  • Payment and billing information
  • Incoterms and service terms (e.g., DDP, DDU, EXW)

1.3 Internet and Technical Data

  • IP address
  • Device type and operating system
  • Browser type and version
  • Website usage data (e.g., pages visited, session duration)
  • Cookies and other online identifiers

1.4 Communications Data

  • Email and SMS/text message contents and metadata
  • Telephone call logs (e.g., time, duration, caller ID)
  • Voicemail and recorded calls (where legally permitted with notice)

2. Legal Basis for Processing

We collect and process your personal information under the following legal bases:

  • Your consent
  • Performance of a contract (e.g., freight forwarding agreement)
  • Compliance with legal obligations (e.g., U.S. Customs regulations)
  • Legitimate business interests (e.g., fraud prevention, service improvement)

3. Use of Information

We use the personal information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To perform logistics and freight forwarding services, including booking, tracking, customs clearance, and final delivery
  • To respond to inquiries, provide quotes, and facilitate transactions
  • To communicate with you by email, telephone, and SMS regarding active or pending shipments
  • To comply with legal and regulatory obligations (e.g., U.S. CBP, FMC, TSA)
  • To improve our services, website functionality, and customer experience
  • To protect our business operations and investigate any suspected illegal activities or violations of our terms

4. Communications: Email, Phone, and SMS

4.1 Consent to Communications

By providing us with your contact information, you consent to receive transactional and service-related communications from us via email, telephone (including calls made using automated dialing systems), and SMS/text message.

Such communications may include, but are not limited to, booking confirmations, customs inquiries, delivery updates, compliance alerts, invoice notices, and service follow-ups.

4.2 Compliance with TCPA and CAN-SPAM

We comply with the Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA) and CAN-SPAM Act. You have the right to:

  • Opt out of non-essential email communications by clicking the "unsubscribe" link included in such emails.
  • Opt out of SMS communications by replying "STOP" to any text message.
  • Request to no longer receive telephone communications by notifying us directly.

Note: Essential communications necessary to complete a service or comply with law (e.g., customs holds, delivery issues) may not be subject to opt-out.

4.3 Call Recording and Monitoring

We may record or monitor telephone calls for quality assurance, training, and legal compliance, as permitted under New York law. Where legally required, you will be notified at the beginning of the call.

5. Disclosure of Personal Information

We may disclose personal information to the following categories of third parties, solely for the purposes outlined in this Policy:

  • Licensed transportation providers (e.g., airlines, shipping lines, trucking carriers)
  • Customs brokers, warehouse operators, and freight terminals
  • U.S. and international government authorities (e.g., CBP, TSA, FDA)
  • Third-party service providers (e.g., IT systems, cloud storage, CRM tools)
  • Legal counsel, auditors, and insurance providers
  • Affiliates and subsidiaries of the Company

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for monetary or commercial gain.

6. Data Security

In compliance with the New York SHIELD Act, we implement and maintain reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of personal information.

These safeguards include:

  • Secure data storage and encryption protocols
  • Access controls and employee training
  • Network and system security monitoring
  • Secure communications (e.g., HTTPS, secure FTP)

While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your data, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure.

7. Data Retention

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including:

  • Completing logistics services
  • Maintaining legal and regulatory compliance
  • Fulfilling accounting, tax, and record-keeping obligations
  • Managing customer support and dispute resolution

Data no longer required is securely deleted, anonymized, or archived in accordance with applicable laws.

8. Cookies and Website Tracking

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to enhance user experience and analyze website performance. You may control cookies through your browser settings.

Types of cookies may include:

  • Strictly necessary cookies (for secure login and site navigation)
  • Performance cookies (for tracking usage statistics)
  • Functional cookies (to remember preferences)

9. Your Rights Under New York Law

Under applicable New York State privacy laws, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you
  • Request correction or deletion of inaccurate data
  • Withdraw consent (where processing is based on consent)
  • Be informed of data breaches involving your information (in accordance with breach notification laws)

To exercise any of the above rights, please contact us using the contact details provided below. We will verify your identity and respond in accordance with applicable law.

10. International Data Transfers

Due to the global nature of our services, your data may be transferred to and processed in countries outside the United States. In such cases, we ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place to protect your data in accordance with applicable laws.

11. Children's Privacy

Our website and services are intended for individuals over the age of eighteen (18). We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under the age of 13. If we become aware that such data has been collected, we will take steps to delete it.

12. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We reserve the right to modify this Policy at any time to reflect changes in law, our data practices, or business operations. If we make material changes, we will update the "Effective Date" above and post the revised policy on our website. Continued use of our services constitutes acceptance of any changes.
